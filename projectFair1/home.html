Criminalization of violence, namely criminal liability for beatings and minor injury to health (so far they are in the Code of Administrative Offenses); Classifying them as criminal offenses of public prosecution; Tougher penalties and exclusion of reconciliation of the parties for all forms of violence against children.
In 2017, domestic violence was decriminalized in Kazakhstan: beatings and minor damage to health, such as bruises, bruises and fractures of limbs, were transferred from the criminal code to the administrative one. After the murder of Saltanat Nukenova, Kazakhstanis again began to draw the attention of the authorities to the problem of domestic violence in the country with special activity. On the Otinish website.kz launched an open letter to Kassym-Jomart Tokayev, in which Kazakhstanis demanded tougher penalties for domestic violence. It was signed by more than 150 thousand people.
In April 2024, Kazakhstani activists and human rights defenders launched a campaign "Write a letter to Parliament" with a request to send a letter supporting the law to the Senate. The authors of the letter were human rights activist Khalida Azhigulova, director of Yerkindik Kanaty Elena Shvetsova, Saltanat Nukenova's brother Aitbek Amangeldy and others. More than five thousand Kazakhstanis supported the action and wrote a letter to the Senate.
In addition, Kazakhstani civil society activists organized rallies in Germany, Italy, Lithuania and the United States in support of the criminalization of domestic violence in Kazakhstan. Thanks to the joint efforts of civil society, a number of international media and organizations have written about the problem of domestic violence in Kazakhstan: Deutsche Welle, Human Rights Watch, Dozhd TV Channel, Reuters and many others.
